## Experiments

Six experiments tested in this session. All regressed; best val_bpb unchanged at **1.0387**.

### Experiment 1: Reduce depth 8 -> 7 (same width 512)

**Rationale:** With ASPECT*RATIO=64 and HEAD*DIM=128, depth=7 gives the same model width (512 dim, 4 heads) but one fewer layer. This trades ~23% of parameters (50.3M -> 38.8M) for ~15% more training steps. Previous experiments tested depth 9 and 10 (both regressed due to fewer steps), but depth 7 hadn't been tested.

| Metric | Value |

|--------|-------|

| val_bpb | **1.0515** ✗ (prev best: 1.0387) |

| Steps | 1538 |

| MFU | 14.43% |

| VRAM | 20,001 MB |

| Tokens | 201.6M |

| Params | 38.8M |

**Status: DISCARD** — Regressed by 0.013. Despite 15% more steps (1538 vs typical 1340) and 15% more tokens (201.6M vs typical 176M), the model couldn't compensate for losing one transformer layer. Training loss at end (~2.93) was similar to 8-layer baseline, but the reduced capacity hurt generalization. Note: this experiment also ran with "last-2-layers VE" instead of alternating VE due to a working-tree state issue, which may have contributed to the regression. Depth map: **7→1.0515**, **8→best**, 9→1.054, 10→1.061.

### Experiment 2: Muon momentum warmup 300 -> 500 steps

**Rationale:** The Muon optimizer warms momentum from 0.85 to 0.95 over the first 300 steps (~22% of training). Extending to 500 steps (~37%) keeps the optimizer more conservative during early training, potentially improving stability during the LR warmup-to-peak transition.

| Steps | 1477 |

| MFU | 15.48% |

| VRAM | 22,806 MB |

| Tokens | 193.6M |

**Status: DISCARD** — Regressed by 0.007. On this fast GPU (15.48% MFU), baseline typically gives ~1.044-1.046, so 1.046 is within normal range. The longer momentum warmup is neutral at best — doesn't help or hurt significantly. The 300-step warmup is sufficient for the optimizer to find its stride. Momentum warmup map: **100→regressed** (session 34), **300→best**, **500→1.046** (neutral/slight regression).

### Experiment 3: Disable EMA (use raw weights after warmdown)

**Rationale:** After full LR warmdown to 0, the raw model weights should be nearly identical to the EMA-averaged weights. If disabling EMA doesn't hurt, it's a simplification win that removes ~50M of EMA state storage and per-step computation.

| Metric | Value |

|--------|-------|

| val_bpb | **1.0424** ✗ (prev best: 1.0387) |

| Steps | 1467 |

| MFU | 15.37% |

| VRAM | 22,806 MB |

| Tokens | 192.3M |

**Status: DISCARD (but PROMISING)** — Technically regressed by 0.004, but this is the most interesting result of the session. At 15.37% MFU, the baseline with EMA typically gives 1.044-1.046. The no-EMA result of 1.042 is actually **better** than typical EMA baseline at comparable GPU speed. This strongly suggests that raw weights after full warmdown to LR=0 may be as good as or better than EMA-averaged weights. The EMA at decay=0.99 averages ~100 steps, introducing some drag from older, less-converged weights. After full warmdown, this averaging is unnecessary or counterproductive. This merits further investigation with repeated runs to control for GPU variance.

### Experiment 6: Sqrt warmdown (cooldown^0.5 instead of linear)

**Rationale:** Sqrt warmdown keeps LR higher for longer during the decay phase and drops steeply at the end. At warmdown midpoint, linear gives 0.5x while sqrt gives 0.707x peak LR. The idea is that the model benefits from more time at moderate LR for continued learning, with rapid final convergence.

| Metric | Value |

|--------|-------|

| val_bpb | **1.0509** ✗ (prev best: 1.0387) |

| Steps | 1338 |

| MFU | 14.01% |

| VRAM | 22,806 MB |

| Tokens | 175.4M |

**Status: DISCARD** — Regressed by 0.012. Training loss at end notably higher (~2.96 vs typical ~2.93). At step 1335 (99.8% progress), lrm was still 0.05, vs ~0.003 for linear. The model never fully converges because LR stays too high too late. The steep final drop in the last 0.2% of training isn't enough time for weights to settle. Warmdown shape map: **sqrt (cooldown^0.5)→1.051 ✗**, **linear (cooldown)→best**, **cosine→regressed** (session 31). Linear warmdown provides the best balance of continued learning and convergence.

## Analysis
