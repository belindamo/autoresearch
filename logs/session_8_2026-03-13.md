Rationale: Session 7 showed that reducing weight decay from 0.2 to 0.1 improved val_bpb (1.0437→1.0420), confirming that regularization was over-constraining the model in short 5-minute runs. This experiment pushes further in the same direction to see if even less regularization helps.

## Analysis

- Reducing weight decay from 0.1 to 0.05 gave a clear improvement (1.0420 → 1.0395)
- Steps increased slightly (1463→1517) and MFU improved (15.34%→15.91%), suggesting less weight decay overhead
- Tokens processed also went up (191.8M→198.8M), likely due to slightly faster steps
- This continues the trend from Session 7: less regularization helps in short training runs
- The cautious weight decay schedule (`WD * (1 - progress)`) decays WD to zero by end of training, so lower WD primarily affects the first half of training where the model is still exploring
- Two consecutive WD reductions (0.2→0.1→0.05) have both improved results — there may be more room to go (0.025 or even 0)
- **Conclusion: 0.05 weight decay is better tuned for 5-minute runs; the model benefits from less regularization during its limited training window**
