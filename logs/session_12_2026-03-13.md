# Session 12 — 2026-03-13

## Experiment

**Increase scalar LR from 0.5 to 0.8**

Rationale: Per-layer scalars (resid*lambdas, x0*lambdas) are cheap parameters that control residual stream mixing. A higher scalar LR may help these gating parameters learn their optimal values faster within the short 5-minute training budget. The x0_params group uses (0.96, 0.95) betas which are already conservative, so a higher LR could compensate.

## Changes

- `SCALAR_LR`: 0.5 → 0.8

## Analysis

- Increasing scalar LR from 0.5 to 0.8 slightly hurt performance (1.0395 → 1.0401)
- The regression is small (0.0006) but consistent — the scalar parameters are already learning at a good rate with LR=0.5
- Higher scalar LR may cause the per-layer mixing coefficients (resid*lambdas, x0*lambdas) to overshoot their optimal values
- Note: resid*params use *`scalarlr * 0.01 = 0.008` and x0*params use *`scalarlr = 0.8` directly, so the x0_lambdas saw the biggest LR increase
- The x0_lambdas (initialized at 0.1) control how much the original embedding is mixed into each layer — too aggressive tuning may destabilize early training
- **Conclusion: scalar_lr=0.5 is well-tuned; do not increase it further**
