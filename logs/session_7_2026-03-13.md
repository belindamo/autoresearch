## Experiment

**Reduce weight decay from 0.2 to 0.1**

Rationale: With only a 5-minute training budget, the model doesn't have enough iterations to overfit significantly. Halving the cautious weight decay from 0.2 to 0.1 allows parameters to grow more freely and fit the data better without the regularization penalty constraining convergence.

- `WEIGHT_DECAY`: 0.2 → 0.1

## Analysis

- Reducing weight decay from 0.2 to 0.1 gave a small but clear improvement (1.0437 → 1.0420)
- Steps (1463), MFU (15.34%), and tokens (191.8M) are very close to Session 5 baseline (1495 steps, 15.68% MFU, 196.0M tokens) — slight differences likely due to run variance
- The improvement confirms that 0.2 weight decay was slightly too aggressive for this short training budget
- The cautious weight decay schedule (`WD * (1 - progress)`) already decays WD to zero by end of training, so the main effect is during mid-training where a lower WD lets parameters explore more freely
- **Conclusion: 0.1 weight decay is better tuned for 5-minute runs; regularization was slightly over-constraining the model**
