## Experiment

**Increase depth from 8 to 10 (ASPECT_RATIO=64 unchanged)**

Rationale: With most hyperparameters well-mapped (LRs, warmup, warmdown, weight decay, betas, softcap all confirmed at their optima), the next lever is model capacity. Depth 10 with ASPECT*RATIO=64 gives model*dim=640, 5 heads, ~85.9M params (up from ~50.3M). The `dmodellrscale` factor automatically adjusts LRs for the wider dimension. The hypothesis was that more capacity could yield lower val_bpb despite fewer training steps.

## Analysis

- The deeper model (85.9M params) only completed 811 steps in 5 minutes, down from ~1340-1560 steps at depth 8 (50.3M params)
- Despite 70% more parameters, the model saw 43% fewer tokens (106M vs 186M), and the reduced training iterations mean the larger model couldn't converge
- The train loss at the end (2.968) was much higher than depth-8 runs (~3.14 at similar BPB), indicating the model hadn't trained enough
- For a 5-minute time budget, depth 8 with 50.3M params is a better compute-quality tradeoff
- This confirms that in the short-training regime, more parameters are not always better — the model needs enough steps to converge
- Lower peak VRAM (34.3 GB vs 45 GB) suggests the depth-8 model was using a larger batch size relative to its parameter count
