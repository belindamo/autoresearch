## Experiment

**Reduce Muon Nesterov momentum target from 0.95 to 0.90**

Rationale: Session 21 showed that higher Adam beta1 (0.9) hurt short-run performance, confirming that lower momentum is better for 5-minute training. The `getmuonmomentum` function ramps Nesterov momentum from 0.85 to 0.95 over 300 steps. Reducing the target to 0.90 follows the same principle — less momentum means faster gradient adaptation, which should help in a short training regime.

Also cleaned up two unlogged commits on the branch (Muon beta2 0.95→0.9, x0_lambda 0.1→0.15) that had been pushed but never tested. A baseline run confirmed they regressed to 1.0472, so both were reverted before running this experiment.

## Analysis

- Reducing Muon momentum target from 0.95 to 0.90 produced val_bpb=1.0470, which regresses vs the reported best of 1.0387
- **Important observation on GPU variance**: Session 15 (which set the 1.0387 record) achieved 1,560 steps and 16.35% MFU, while most other runs get ~1,340 steps and ~14% MFU. This ~16% throughput difference translates to ~29M more training tokens (204.5M vs 175.4M), which directly lowers val_bpb
- The consistent "baseline" performance at ~1,340 steps appears to be ~1.047, not 1.0387. The 1.0387 may partly reflect a favorable GPU allocation rather than purely the warmup change
- Comparing this experiment (1.0470, 1338 steps) to the baseline at similar throughput (1.0472, 1361 steps), the momentum change is essentially neutral (Δ=-0.0002, within noise)
- **Conclusion**: Muon momentum target of 0.90 vs 0.95 makes no meaningful difference at this throughput level. The optimizer's Nesterov momentum is less sensitive than Adam's beta1 because Muon's polar express orthogonalization dominates the gradient direction signal.
