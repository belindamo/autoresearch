## Experiment

**Increase Adam beta1 from 0.8 to 0.9**

Rationale: Higher momentum (beta1) for AdamW-optimized parameters (embeddings, unembedding, value embeddings, scalars) could help smooth noisy gradient estimates, potentially improving convergence in short training runs.

## Analysis

- Increasing Adam beta1 from 0.8 to 0.9 significantly hurt performance (1.0387 → 1.0517, +0.0130)
- Higher momentum makes the optimizer too sluggish for the 5-minute training regime
- With only ~1340 steps, lower beta1=0.8 allows faster adaptation to changing gradient landscapes
- The embedding and unembedding parameters need rapid adjustment early in training; higher momentum delays this
- **Conclusion: ADAM_BETAS=(0.8, 0.95) is well-calibrated. Lower beta1 is better for short training runs.**
