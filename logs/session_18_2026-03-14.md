# Session 18 — 2026-03-14

## Experiment

**Increase embedding LR from 1.0 to 1.5**

Rationale: Session 4 showed clear improvement going from 0.6→1.0 for embedding LR. The hypothesis is that embeddings benefit from even more aggressive learning in short training runs, allowing faster token representation learning. Simple, single-parameter change.

## Changes

- `EMBEDDING_LR`: 1.0 → 1.5

**Status: DISCARD** — val*bpb regressed by 0.0088, reverted to EMBEDDING*LR=1.0.

## Analysis

- Increasing embedding LR from 1.0 to 1.5 significantly hurt performance (1.0387 → 1.0475, +0.0088)
- The 50% LR increase caused embedding instability — the embeddings learned too aggressively and likely overshot good representations early in training
- Throughput dropped slightly (1338 steps vs ~1516 at baseline), and MFU was lower (14.01% vs ~15.89%), suggesting some overhead or instability
- The 0.6→1.0 improvement in Session 4 was a 67% increase that worked, but 1.0→1.5 (another 50%) overshoots
- Note: the embedding LR also applies to value embeddings (both use `embeddinglr`* in *`setupoptimizer`), so this change affects both wte and value_embeds — the compounded effect may have made it worse
- **Conclusion: EMBEDDING_LR=1.0 is optimal. Going higher causes instability; the embedding LR parameter space is now explored (0.6 < 1.0 > 1.5).**
