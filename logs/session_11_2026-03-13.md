# Session 11 — 2026-03-13

## Experiment

**Increase warmdown ratio from 0.67 to 0.75**

Rationale: Session 6 showed that *reducing* warmdown from 0.67 to 0.5 hurt convergence (1.0557 vs 1.0437). Since longer cooldown periods are beneficial, increasing warmdown to 0.75 might give the optimizer more time to settle into a good minimum. This dedicates 75% of the time budget to LR decay, leaving only 23% for peak LR training.

- `WARMDOWN_RATIO`: 0.67 → 0.75

## Analysis

- Increasing warmdown from 0.67 to 0.75 slightly hurt performance (1.0395 → 1.0401)
- The regression is small (0.0006) but consistent with the idea that 0.67 is near-optimal
- With warmdown=0.75, only ~23% of training time is at peak LR (after 2% warmup), which may be too little time at full learning rate for the model to learn effectively
- Combined with session 6 (warmdown 0.67→0.5 also hurt), the evidence suggests **0.67 is a well-tuned warmdown ratio** — both increasing and decreasing it hurts
- The warmdown schedule is now fully explored: 0.5 ✗, 0.67 ✓, 0.75 ✗
- **Conclusion: warmdown_ratio=0.67 is optimal; do not change it further**
