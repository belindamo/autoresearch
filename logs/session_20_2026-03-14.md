# Session 20 — 2026-03-14

## Experiment

**Increase FINAL*****LR*****FRAC from 0.0 to 0.01**

Rationale: With WARMDOWN_RATIO=0.67, the LR anneals all the way to 0 during the final 67% of training. This means the very last training steps have near-zero learning rate and make essentially no progress. A small non-zero final LR (1% of initial) would keep the model learning throughout, potentially squeezing out extra improvement at zero complexity cost.

## Analysis

- Setting FINAL*LR*FRAC=0.01 hurt performance (1.0387 → 1.0472, +0.0085)
- The non-zero final LR prevents the model from fully "settling" during the warmdown phase
- With cosine-like annealing to 0, the model benefits from the gradual parameter stabilization — even tiny updates at the end can disrupt convergence
- The warmdown schedule effectively acts as a form of model averaging/stabilization; maintaining a 1% residual LR breaks this
- **Conclusion: FINAL*****LR*****FRAC=0.0 is optimal. Full annealing to zero is important for the warmdown phase to function as intended.**
