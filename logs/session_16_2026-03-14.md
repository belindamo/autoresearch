# Session 16 — 2026-03-14

## Experiment

**Increase warmup ratio from 0.05 to 0.08**

Rationale: Session 15 improved val_bpb by increasing warmup from 0.02 to 0.05. Continuing the trend — a longer warmup gives the Muon optimizer even more time to build accurate momentum estimates before hitting full learning rate. Simple, single-parameter change continuing a proven direction.

## Changes

- `WARMUP_RATIO`: 0.05 → 0.08

**Status: DISCARD** — val*bpb regressed by 0.0113, reverted to warmup*ratio=0.05.

## Analysis

- Increasing warmup from 5% to 8% significantly hurt performance (1.0387 → 1.0500, +0.0113)
- The longer warmup consumed too much of the 5-minute training budget at sub-optimal learning rates, reducing effective training steps from 1560 to 1332
- MFU also dropped from 16.35% to 13.95%, and total tokens processed fell from 204.5M to 174.6M
- The warmup phase uses low learning rates that contribute little to learning — 8% is too much time spent "ramping up" in a short training run
- **Conclusion: warmup_ratio=0.05 is the sweet spot; both lower (0.02) and higher (0.08) are worse. The optimizer needs some warmup but not too much in a 5-minute budget.**
