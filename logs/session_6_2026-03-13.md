# Session 6 — 2026-03-13

## Experiment

**Reduce warmdown ratio from 0.67 to 0.5**

Rationale: With warmdown_ratio=0.67, the model spends 67% of training in LR decay and only ~31% at peak LR. Reducing to 0.5 gives 48% at peak LR — more time for the optimizer to learn at full strength before decay.

## Changes

- `WARMDOWN_RATIO`: 0.67 → 0.5

**Status: DISCARD** — val_bpb regressed significantly (+0.012 worse).

## Analysis

- Reducing warmdown hurt across the board: fewer steps (1321 vs 1495), lower MFU (13.83% vs 15.68%), fewer tokens (173.1M vs 196.0M)
- The long warmdown (67%) appears crucial — it provides a gradual LR decay that helps the model converge in the final phase
- With a shorter warmdown, the decay is steeper, and the model has less time to settle into a good minimum
- The extra time at peak LR did not compensate for the loss of gradual cooldown
- **Conclusion: 0.67 warmdown ratio is well-tuned for the 5-minute budget; do not reduce it**
