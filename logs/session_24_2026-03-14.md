# Session 24 — 2026-03-14

## Experiment

**Reduce warmdown ratio from 0.67 to 0.60**

Rationale: Session 6 showed warmdown=0.5 was too aggressive (1.0557), Session 11 showed warmdown=0.75 was too high (1.0401). 0.60 is between the current optimal (0.67) and the known-bad (0.5), giving more time at peak LR before cooldown. The hypothesis was that the additional ~7% of training time at peak LR (vs warmdown) could let the optimizer make more progress before cooldown.

Note: First attempt ran on `master` branch by mistake (missing `--branch` arg), yielding val_bpb=1.121 with only 345 steps — a much larger model on different hyperparameters. Re-ran correctly on `autoresearch/sundial`.

## Analysis

- Reducing warmdown ratio from 0.67 to 0.60 regressed to 1.0491, worse than both the best (1.0387) and the typical-throughput baseline (~1.047)
- This conclusively maps the warmdown sensitivity: 0.50 → 1.0557 ✗, 0.60 → 1.0491 ✗, **0.67 → best** ✓, 0.75 → 1.0401 ✗
- The warmdown ratio 0.67 is a robust optimum — both higher and lower values within ±0.10 produce worse results
- The model needs substantial warmdown time for the LR schedule to converge properly. With 0.60, the warmdown starts too late and doesn't fully anneal before the time budget expires
- At 1356 steps / 14.20% MFU this was a typical throughput run, so the regression is real (not a GPU variance artifact)
